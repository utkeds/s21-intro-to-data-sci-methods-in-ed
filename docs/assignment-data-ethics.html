Assignment - Data Ethics

Personal Data Ethics Statement

Overview

Write a personal statement on one’s perspective toward the role and nature of data, addressing how issues of equity, privacy, and ethics are central to data science in education.

Background

Required reading

  • Required reading - Data Feminism, c1: https://data-feminism.mitpress.mit.edu/pub/frfa9szd/release/3

Optional readings/resources to consult

  • An argument for a Hippocratic Oath for data scientists
  • Manifest-No
  • Ethical Guidelines for the Association of Internet Researchers
  • Doing Data for Good Right
  • Sharing Our Views on Ethics: DataKind UK Releases Survey Results from Community
  • ROpenSci’s policies on data, ethics, and human subjects research

Details

  • Write a one-two page (single-spaced; if double-spaced, then two-four pages) articulation of your ideas and commitments regarding data ethics; this will be a draft; please use Google Docs, as you’ll submit a Google Docs link later
  • Include at least one paragraph on each of the following:
  • An overview of your work (especially your research) and what kinds of data you use in it
  • Why data ethics can matter where it comes to your work
  • What principles (three or more) you wish to aspire to in your data analysis and data science-related work (for examples, consider the seven core principles from the Data Femgit inism, c1 reading, as well as the optional resources/resources); consider principles that outline what you will do as well as what you will not do (see Manifest-No for refusals of specific kinds of data practices)
  • Include three or more citations to publications or other resources (websites are okay); these can include any of the above resources as well as others that you identify or those with which you are already familiar
  • Recall that this is a draft; you will next read three of your peers’ data ethics statements and provide comments on them; you will then revise your statement
  • Then, near the end of the semester, you will create your final statement

Timeline and Due Dates

  • Draft due by Thursday, 3/4
  • Reading and providing feedback on peer’s statements, 3/11
  • Revising your statement based on your peer’s feedback, 3/18
  • Final statement due by Thursday, 4/22

How to submit

  • Please submit a Google Docs link for this assignment on Canvas. Also, please post that link to the #data-ethics-statement channel on Slack (you may have to browse and then add the channel to access it). Be sure that the sharing settings are such that anyone with the link can comment on the document.
